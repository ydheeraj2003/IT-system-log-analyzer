Why in news?

Operation Rakshak is an ongoing counter-insurgency and counter-terrorism operation started during the height of insurgency in Jammu and Kashmir in June 1990. The operation is in the news because it is one of the longest-running military operations in Indian history. It is also one of the most successful counter-insurgency operations in the world.

Why was project launched?

Operation Rakshak was launched to counter the insurgency in Jammu and Kashmir, which had been supported by Pakistan. The insurgency had caused widespread violence and instability in the region. The Indian government was determined to crush the insurgency and restore peace and stability to Jammu and Kashmir.

What does the project do?

Operation Rakshak involves a wide range of activities, including:
  • Cordon and search operations
  • Night patrols
  • Ambushes
  • Intelligence gathering
  • Civic action programs

The Indian Army has deployed a large number of troops to Jammu and Kashmir to carry out Operation Rakshak. The Army has also worked closely with the state police and other security forces to counter the insurgency. Operation Rakshak has been very successful in reducing violence and instability in Jammu and Kashmir. The number of militant attacks has fallen significantly since the operation was launched. The operation has also helped to improve the security situation in the region, which has led to an increase in economic activity and tourism.
