Why in news?

Operation Sarp Vinash (Operation Destroy Serpents) was a counterterrorism operation undertaken by the Indian Army from April to May 2003 in the Surankote area of the Pir Panjal range in Jammu and Kashmir. The operation was in the news because it was one of the most successful counterterrorism operations conducted by the Indian Army.

Why was project launched?

Operation Sarp Vinash was launched to flush out terrorists who had made bases in the Hilkaka Poonch-Surankot area of the Pir Panjal range. The terrorists were using the area as a base to launch attacks against Indian security forces and civilians. The Indian government was determined to crush the terrorist threat in the area and restore peace and stability to the region. Operation Sarp Vinash was launched to achieve this objective.

What does the project do?

Operation Sarp Vinash involved a large-scale deployment of Indian Army troops to the Surankote area. The Army launched a series of cordon and search operations, ambushes, and night patrols to flush out the terrorists. The Army also used air support to target terrorist hideouts and supply lines. The operation was carried out over a period of several weeks and was very successful in eliminating the terrorist presence in the area. Operation Sarp Vinash resulted in the killing of 65 terrorists and the capture of a large number of weapons and ammunition. The operation also helped to clear the area of terrorist infrastructure, such as training camps and hideouts. Operation Sarp Vinash was a major victory for the Indian Army in its fight against terrorism. The operation showed that the Army was capable of conducting successful counterterrorism operations in difficult terrain. The operation also helped to restore peace and stability to the Surankote area.

  • To protect the lives of civilians
  • To promote peace and development in the Surankote area
  • To build trust and confidence between the Army and the local population
